About the Venue

London Museum Docklands traces the 2,000-year history of London's river, port, and people, from the arrival of the Romans to the rise of Canary Wharf. Located over five floors within a splendid late Georgian warehouse, it is Canary Wharf's only Grade I listed venue.


The venue offers a remarkable range of event spaces, from intimate boardrooms for 16 guests to the full venue hire accommodating up to 1,500. With original pine columns, exposed brickwork, and stunning waterside views, every space combines heritage character with modern AV and catering capabilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum capacity of London Museum Docklands?
The full venue accommodates up to 1,500 guests for a reception. Individual spaces range from 16 (boardroom) to 400 (Muscovado Hall reception).
How much does it cost to hire London Museum Docklands?
Venue fees start from Â£820 for the Docklands Boardroom and go up to Â£11,700 for whole venue hire. Most mid-size spaces range from Â£1,300 to Â£2,450.
What catering options are available?
The venue has an in-house caterer providing bespoke menus for all event types. From working lunches and canapes to multi-course dinners and summer BBQs on the private terrace.
How do I get to London Museum Docklands?
The museum is at West India Quay, a 2-minute walk from West India Quay DLR or 5 minutes from Canary Wharf (Jubilee line and Elizabeth line).
Can I combine multiple spaces?
Yes. Spaces can be hired individually or combined. Popular combinations include Wilberforce + Quayside for conferences with breakouts, and the full ground floor (Muscovado Hall + Riverside + Sugar Store) for large receptions.
