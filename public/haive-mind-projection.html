Why These Numbers Are Realistic

  • Recipes are cached forever — agents download once, never return. No recurring consumption like Spotify.
  • APIs rarely change — most recipes stay valid for months. Weak "freshness" value prop.
  • Small initial TAM — only AI agent operators who need service recipes (hundreds, not millions).
  • Open source — community growth is slow and organic.

Risk Factors

Recipe Caching = No Recurring Usage

Unlike SaaS where users return daily, recipes are "download once, cache forever." This fundamentally limits direct monetisation potential.

APIs Don't Change Often

Most REST APIs are stable for months/years. The "freshness guarantee" subscription model is weak because there's not enough churn to justify ongoing payments.

Open Source Can Be Forked

If we later try to monetise aggressively, community could fork. Need to balance openness with sustainability.

Small Initial TAM

AI agent operators who need service recipes = hundreds initially, not millions. Growth will be slow until agent adoption explodes.

Security Risk

Malicious recipes could compromise agents. Guardian mitigates but not 100%. One security incident could kill trust.

Low Conversion Rates

Open source typically sees 1-5% paid conversion. Even with 1,000 users, that's 10-50 paying customers max.
