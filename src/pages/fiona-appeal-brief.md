## The two grounds of appeal

### Ground 1 — Deliberate undermining of CED

- Target raised +106% with no business case.
- Inbound AE + 360 pipelines cut at the same time.
- "7% product" forced as substitute, untested, later admitted as failed.
- External factors ignored (Carnilove £6K delay; EFG Hermes £42.15K delay due to venue ownership change + force majeure renegotiation post-Middle East war).
- Profitability data requested by Fiona, never provided.
- Constitutes **constructive unfair dismissal**.

### Ground 2 — Unlawful redundancy rationale and process

- Company itself admits the role's core functions remain required (offered freelance rehire).
- Therefore **fails the statutory test in s.139(1) ERA 1996**: no cessation or diminishment of work.
- Process defects:
  - At-risk notice not given in writing before consultation began (ACAS Code breach).
  - 3 consultations in 6 working days = not genuine consultation.
  - CED excluded from 9 April Q2 strategy = pre-determined outcome.

## 4 questions for the appeal officer (Will)

These are the questions you can put on the table if there is space, or feed to Fiona for her to ask. Each one forces them onto evidence they don't have.

1. **"Can the Company produce the P&L and operating cost data that Fiona requested in March, which would substantiate the claim that CED has not been profitable each year?"**
   *(If yes, where is it. If no, the entire redundancy rationale collapses.)*

2. **"What was the business case for raising the CED target by 106% in 2026, and was that target adjusted when the AE and 360 referral pipelines were withdrawn?"**
   *(Either there's no case, or the targets were knowingly unachievable. Either answer hurts them.)*

3. **"If the Company intends to engage Fiona — or another freelancer — to continue delivering events after her redundancy, how does that reconcile with the statutory definition of redundancy under s.139(1) ERA 1996, which requires the work itself to have ceased or diminished?"**
   *(This is the legal kill-shot. There is no good answer.)*

4. **"Why was Fiona, as Director of Production & Delivery, excluded from the 9 April away day Q2 roadmap presentation, given that the consultation process did not formally begin until 13 April?"**
   *(Forces them to admit the decision pre-dated the process, or to invent a reason that will not stand up later.)*

## Signals to watch for from Will / Rhiannon

- **Concession-by-omission:** if they don't push back on a specific allegation, that's a tacit concession. Note it.
- **New justification:** any new rationale that wasn't in the dismissal letter is **post-hoc** and weakens their position significantly.
- **Process offer:** if they offer to "review", "re-consider", or "appoint an external reviewer" — that's progress; accept and confirm in writing.
- **Settlement language:** anything resembling *"how can we resolve this"* / *"what outcome are you looking for"* — Fiona should defer to Forsters before answering.
